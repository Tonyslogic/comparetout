Help: Inverters

The inverter is an essential component if you have solar panels and or a battery. The inverter transforms electricity from one form to another depending on a number of factors. Without an inverter configured, the app will not allow adding solar panels or batteries. Multiple inverters are supported by the app. In practice you need to get professional advice on a multi-inverter system.

In edit mode, inverters can be added (button in bottom right), and deleted (menu)

The inverter has seven entries:

EntryDescription
Inverter nameUsed to identify a specific inverter when connecting panels or batteries.
Minimum excess (kW)The level of clear excess generation needed before battery charging will occur.
Maximum load(kW)The output rating of the inverter.
Number of MPPTSolar inverters include ports (Maximum Power Point Tracking) to attach strings of inverters. This is useful when you have different strings with different orientations. This is used when attaching panels to the inverter.
AC to DC Loss (%)When charging from the grid, the inverter must convert from AD to DC. No inverter is perfect, so there will be loss. This loss is normally well documented. It you don't know then stick with the default.
DC to AC Loss (%)When providing electricity to the house load, the inverter must convert from DC to AC. No inverter is perfect, so there will be loss. This loss is normally well documented. It you don't know then stick with the default.
DC to DC Loss (%)Some inverters (Hybrid) can bypass AC when there is a viable excess and a connected battery has capacity. The inverter still has a little work to do to match the battery and PV DC and no inverter is perfect, so there will be loss. This loss is normally well documented. It you don't know then stick with the default.
