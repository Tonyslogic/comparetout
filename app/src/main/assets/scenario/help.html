This is the one stop shop for all thing usage what-if scenario related.

From here you can specify the characteristics of a what-if scenario, and once simulations have completed, explore the results on a daily monthly or annual basis.

There are four tabs in this area of the app. The first provides a launch point for the usage what-if scenario specification. The other three graphical views of the simulation output that help in understanding how the scenario works.

There are six main configuration points for a usage what-if scenario:

IconDescription

Domestic usage

 The only mandatory configuration in the usage what-if scenario. This item captures how electricity is used. There are several sources that can help with the population of this data. Note that this should not include usage of an immersion, or EV. Heat-pumps should as there is as yet no other means to add this to the scenario. The more accurate this data, the more accurate will be the simulation. A green tick indicates that house load has been defined.

Inverter

 The inverter is used to connect one or both solar panels and/or batteries to the house. Houses use AC electricity. Panels and batteries use DC. Conversion between the two is not perfect, and manufacturers have minor differences. If you don't know the figures for you inverter, the defaults will provide a reasonable result. More accurate data, the more accurate will be the simulation. A green tick indicates that at least one inverter has been defined.

Panels

 Panels convert light to electricity. They are connected to inverters. This area captures the vital statistics for panels, and how they are arranged. Once configured, a green tick will be present on the panel button. The app must also have data for the amount of light expected. There are various sources of this data. When this data is present, the panel will have a small sun symbol in the top left. Usage what-if scenarios with configured panels but no solar data are not eligible for simulation.

Panels

 Batteries can be used to store electricity for later use. Batteries can be charged from excess (beyond the instant need of the house load) solar or scheduled from the grid. Batteries must be connected to an inverter. You cannot configure batteries until an inverter is configured. Only one battery per inverter is supported. Once configured a green settings icon will appear under the battery button. If purchase shifting (charging from the grid is enabled, a clock icon will also be visible.

Water

 Water is an excellent energy store -- though it will cool over time if not used. Hot water is also useful to have in a domestic environment -- though this utility is subjective. There are products that will monitor for excess solar generation (beyond the instant need of the house load), and divert that to a hot water immersion heater. On some time of use tariffs, it is more cost effective to use electricity to heat water, the ability to schedule is important. This area covers three aspects of hot water: system configuration; scheduling; diversion. When present in the usage what-if scenario icons for settings, clock and divert icons will be visible under the water button.

EV

 Domestic charging of EVs is an important use of excess solar generation (beyond the instant need of the house load). Of course solar is not always sufficient, the ability to schedule is needed. When EV charge schedules or diversions are configured, clock and divert icons will be visible under the EV button.

The lower part of the screen is populated when the usage what-if scenario has been simulated and costed. It provides a quick overview of:

  • The cheapest supplier and plan (cost) for this usage what-if scenario
  • Self consumption (pie, top left). How much of the generated electricity was used locally, and how much was exported.
  • Self supplied (pie, top right). How much of the total load was serviced by local generation, and grid import.
  • Load breakdown. How much of the overall load came form EV charging, Hot water heating and general domestic use. If there is no local generation, only this pie chart will be visible.
  • Distribution of self generation (pie, bottom left). Where did the local generation go. Destinations are: export; EV; Hot water; battery; domestic load.

Editing (menu) the overview page can be used to specify the name of the usage what-if scenario.

Sharing (menu) is used to share or backup a usage what-if scenario.
