Help: Battery settings

Tutorial on YouTube

The battery is an optional component if you have solar panels. The battery stores electricity for later use. Batteries must be configured before purchase shifting schedules can be created. Multiple batteries are supported by the app, but only a single battery for each inverter..

In edit mode, batteries can be added (button in bottom right), and deleted (menu)

The battery settings page has nine entries:

EntryDescription
Connected Inverter nameUsed to identify the specific inverter. This is needed to ensure that the batteries are charged by the correct panel string first.
Battery size (kWh)The usable capacity of the battery.
Discharge stop (%)At what point should the inverter stop discharging the battery. There are two reasons to do this: (1) you want to prolong the life of the battery and (2) you want ensure there is some capacity in the battery in case of a grid outage.
Max discharge in 5 minutes (kWh)Batteries have physical/chemical limits in terms of how fast they can provide electricity. This value is used in the simulation to determine how much should be drawn from the grid.
Max charge in 5 minutes (kWh)Batteries have physical/chemical limits in terms of how fast they can charge. This value is used in the simulation to determine how much should of the generation excess can be diverted to the battery and how much should be drawn from the grid when charging is scheduled.
Storage Loss (%)Batteries lose charge when not in use, and there is a cost associated with converting electrical to chemical energy. Normally this is not a large number.
Charge rates @... (%)To keep things simple, the battery settings include the Battery Management System (BMS). The BMS has one role in life: to protect the battery. The Charge rates provide a very simple model to describe the behaviour of the BMS at various battery states of charge (SOC). Typically batteries will have charging throttled when near empty or full. These values are used to modify the 'Max charge' figure during simulation.
