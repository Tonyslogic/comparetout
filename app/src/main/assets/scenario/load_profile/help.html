Help: Load profile

Tutorial on YouTube

The load profile is the only mandatory part of a usage what-if scenario. It consists of a basic profile, and how electricity usage is distributed by hour, day of week and month of year. The idea being that as creatures of habit we follow daily, weekly and seasonal patterns that can be used to provide a simple but effective simulation input.

Each year ESBN and the CRU publish "Standard Load Profiles". The standard load profiles are a statistical average of all users categorised by meter type. These are included as options for specifying distributions. Your basic profile is still needed. You can learn more about standard load profiles here.

If you have a smart meter (and do not yet export), ESBN also provide (once you register) a Harmonised Data Format (HDF) file from which your load profile may be extracted (coming soon).

In future additional sources of load profiles (inverter data and Home Assistant) will be added.

The profile has only five entries:

EntryDescription
Distribution sourceSelect an option for how the distribution of electricity is specified.
Annual usageThe typical annual usage of electricity. This should be easily retrieved from electricity bills.
Hourly base loadMost houses have a base load. Fridge, freezer, standby devices, internet routers, etc. If you don't know what yours is, don't worry the default 300W is about right.
Grid import maxMost domestic grid connections have a max rating of 15kW. If you draw more than this fuses will blow and switches will trip. The simulator uses this to curtail the draw when combinations of load profile, hot water schedule and ev charge schedule temporarily exceed the limit.
Grid export maxTo protect the grid from imbalance, ESBN limit the amount of electricity that can be exported. The limit depends on your connection. In Ireland the normal domestic limit is ~5kW. There are exceptions .
